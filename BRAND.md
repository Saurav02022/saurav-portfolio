GitHub, LinkedIn, and the résumé read as the same person.

> survive real conditions — weak networks, flaky devices, async vendor APIs.

Everything on the site should either state this (hero), evidence it
(experience bullets, case studies), or echo it (About, Work intro). If a line
does none of those, cut it.

## Voice rules (applies to every surface)

- Plain-engineer: confident, understated, specific. Real technology, real
  problem, real outcome.
- Banned: passionate, seamless, cutting-edge, robust, innovative, leverage,
  delve, harness, empower, craft; adjective triples; mission statements.
- Numbers only when defensible at a whiteboard; ranges over flattering
  endpoints (say "5–10 minutes", not "5 minutes (87%)").
- When a claim can't be evidenced, say so plainly ("It began as a take-home,
  so correctness is the outcome I can claim honestly").
